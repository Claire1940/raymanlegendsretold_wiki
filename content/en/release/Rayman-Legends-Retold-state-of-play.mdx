<Section icon={Sparkles} title="3D Visuals and Narrative Reimagining">

The most striking change discussed during the **Rayman Legends Retold state of play** presentation is the transition to 3D visuals. While the core gameplay remains rooted in side-scrolling excellence, the world is now rendered with depth that allows the camera to tilt, pan, and zoom. This "playground bending" approach adds a sense of scale previously unseen in the series.

Brand Producer Loïc Gounon emphasized that this reimagining preserves the "world logic" fans love while expanding the lore. The game features fully voiced cinematics, giving Rayman and his friends distinct personalities beyond their traditional grunts and cheers. This dramatization serves to make the stakes feel higher as you battle to save the Glade of Dreams from a shadowy villain spreading chaos across the realms.

<Section icon={Shield} title="Technical Specifications and Enhanced Audio">

The technical leap in Rayman Legends Retold is substantial. By utilizing modern hardware like the PS5 and Xbox Series X, the game achieves a level of detail in its 3D environments that was previously impossible. This includes enhanced lighting, particle effects for magical abilities, and seamless transitions between gameplay and cinematics.

The audio experience is equally prioritized. The legendary duo of Christophe Héral and Grant Kirkhope have returned to expand the soundtrack. The music isn't just background noise; it is deeply integrated into the world logic, reacting to player actions and environmental changes.

**Audio & Technical Features:**

- **Dynamic Soundtrack**: Music evolves based on your performance and location.
