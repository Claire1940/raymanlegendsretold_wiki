<Section icon={Swords} title="Gameplay Innovations and Co-op Chaos">
The core gameplay of Rayman Legends Retold builds upon the acclaimed mechanics of the original while introducing significant innovations. Players will still leap, glide, and punch their way through enemies, but with a renewed sense of scale and dynamic interaction.

| Gameplay Element     | Original Legends (2013)                                      | Retold (2026) Improvements                                                               |
| :------------------- | :----------------------------------------------------------- | :--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Visuals**          | 2D side-scrolling                                            | 2.5D/3D, dynamic camera, integrated 3D elements                                          |
| **Murfy Mechanics**  | Contextual button prompts (PS3/Xbox 360/PC), touch control (Wii U/Vita/PS4) | Fully integrated 3D segments, enhanced co-op actions, more critical to progression       |
| **Musical Levels**   | Rhythm-based stages, covers of popular songs                 | Four brand-new musical stages, more intricate beat-sync mechanics                        |
| **Co-op Play**       | Up to 4 players local co-op                                  | Up to 4 players local co-op with improved interactions, competitive elements like Lums racing |
| **Kung Foot**        | Local multiplayer mini-game                                  | New and improved Kung Foot with enhanced controls, power-ups, customizable rules         |

The game supports up to four players in couch co-op, injecting a new level of fun and chaos into the adventure. Whether you're rescuing Teensies, racing for Lums, or strategically knocking friends off platforms, the shared experience is designed to be memorable. The popular mini-game, Kung Foot, also returns with improved controls, power-ups, and customizable rules, ensuring endless competitive fun.

<Callout type="info">
The seamless integration of 3D elements in the 2D environments, particularly in boss battles and monster encounters, is a standout improvement, enhancing the visual richness and dramatization.
