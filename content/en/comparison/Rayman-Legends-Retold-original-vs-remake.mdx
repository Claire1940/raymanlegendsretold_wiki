<Section icon={Target} title="Performance and Modernization">
Rayman Legends Retold benefits significantly from being built in the Snowdrop engine, the same technology powering other modern Ubisoft titles. This engine allows for higher fidelity graphics and optimized performance across various platforms, including the upcoming Nintendo Switch 2, where the game is targeting a solid 60 frames per second. This commitment to performance ensures a fluid and enjoyable experience, especially for a fast-paced platformer.

<Callout type="info" title="Cross-Platform Availability">

The developers have also implemented quality-of-life tweaks, such as a more navigable overworld, which streamlines the player experience. <Section icon={Shield} title="Why a Remake? Distinguishing Retold from the Original">
Some might question the necessity of remaking a game as acclaimed as the 2013 Rayman Legends. However, Rayman Legends Retold carves out its own identity by offering a fresh perspective on a beloved classic. It's not just about making the game "look better" in 3D; it's about leveraging new technology to enhance the whimsical charm and dynamic gameplay.

<Callout type="warning" title="Not a 3D Platformer">

</Callout>

The additional content, including the new musical levels, expanded Kung Foot, and dragon-riding sequences, provides compelling reasons for both newcomers and veterans to dive back into Rayman's world.
