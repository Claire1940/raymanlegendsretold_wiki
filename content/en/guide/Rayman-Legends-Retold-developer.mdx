<Section icon={Shield} title="The Core Development Team Behind Rayman Legends Retold">
The highly anticipated platformer, Rayman Legends Retold, is being brought to life by a collaborative effort, with **Ubisoft Montpellier** leading the charge. This studio is no stranger to the Rayman universe, having created the original game and many of its predecessors. They are working alongside **Ubisoft Milan**, ensuring a blend of seasoned expertise and fresh perspectives.

The decision to remake Rayman Legends, a game widely considered to have aged gracefully, was driven by a desire to introduce new narrative depth and visual immersion. Developers aimed to retool the Rayman universe, creating possibilities for more focused and immersive storytelling. This includes voiced cinematics and character interactions, a feature not seen since *Rayman 3* over two decades ago.

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Technological Advancements and Visual Redesign">
Rayman Legends Retold leverages Ubisoft's powerful **Snowdrop engine**, famously used for titles like *Star Wars Outlaws* and *The Division 2*. This engine allows for the transition from the original's beautiful hand-drawn 2D visuals to a fully 3D environment, while preserving the whimsical fantasy of the original. The developers meticulously rebuilt backgrounds in 3D, making environments feel more alive and physical.

The visual overhaul extends to character designs, with subtle yet noticeable redesigns for Rayman, Globox, Barbara, and the Teensies. These changes aim to add more detail, texture, and physicality, enhancing the immersive quality of the world.

| Character | Original Design | Retold Design | Key Visual Changes |
| :-------- | :-------------- | :------------ | :----------------- |
| **Rayman** | Classic 2D | 3D Model | Separated eyes, redesigned shoes, tied hoodie strings |
| **Globox** | Smooth texture | Detailed texture | More visible skin texture and detail |
| **Barbara** | Stylized 2D | Fully 3D | Significant glow-up, better suited for 3D |
| **Teensies** | Simple features | Detailed features | Detailed teeth, larger eyes |
| **Betilla** | Origins style | Near-human | Closer to human appearance, controversial redesign |

<Callout type="warning" title="Character Redesign Note">
While most character redesigns aim for enhanced detail, some, like Betilla, have undergone more significant changes that might be divisive among long-time fans. The team, however, views these as deliberate creative choices.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Swords} title="New Gameplay Features and Expanded Content">
Beyond the visual enhancements, Rayman Legends Retold introduces a wealth of new content and gameplay mechanics. The game features four brand new music levels and a completely reworked hub area, with each world now having its own unique visual space and hidden secrets.

A significant addition is the **dragon flights**, new 3D gameplay sequences reminiscent of classic Rayman riding sections. These segments connect different regions of the world, offering a distinct gameplay style from the traditional 2D platforming. The **Land of the Livid Dead** also receives an extended version, almost feeling like a new world with five fresh levels and a "power of light" mechanic.

<Section icon={Trophy} title="Narrative Deep Dive and Enhanced Audio Experience">
A major focus for the Rayman Legends Retold developer team was to imbue the game with a stronger narrative identity. For the first time since *Rayman 3*, Rayman, Globox, Barbara, and Grand Minimus will interact with each other in voiced cutscenes, making them feel more like actual characters rather than just avatars. David Gasman returns as the voice of Rayman, and notably, Billy West reprises his role as Murphy.

The game's soundtrack is also receiving a significant expansion, featuring 55 minutes of new music composed by Christophe Héral and Grant Kirkhope (known for *Banjo-Kazooie* and *Mario + Rabbids*). This blend of original themes and new compositions promises an enriched auditory experience.
